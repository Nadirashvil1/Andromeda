# 🌌 Andromeda

> *"This is not a game about religion. It is a game about a man. The religion is simply the language the house speaks."*

**Andromeda** is a psychological horror RPG built in Godot, in which a masked man wanders into a house that feels like home — but cannot be certain it is. As he explores, the house returns his memories in fragments: the person who destroyed him, and the person who healed him. He does not yet know which was which.

At the center of the house, God and the Devil share a space — not as enemies, but as two old friends who built something together. They are not fighting over his soul. They are watching, with patience and quiet affection, to see what answer he arrives at on his own.

---

## About the Game

**Genre:** Psychological Horror / Narrative RPG  
**Engine:** Godot  
**Status:** In Development

Andromeda is about one question, carried by one man his entire life:

*What is truly good, and what is truly bad — and is there a difference at all?*

The horror here is not violent. It is the horror of recognition — of walking through a room and feeling something you cannot name. Of seeing a face in a painting that looks back. Of remembering something you spent years trying to forget.

**Emotional register:** Dread · Grief · Wonder · A strange, aching peace

**Influences:**
- *Silent Hill 2* — horror that comes from within, not without
- *Disco Elysium* — a broken man reconstructing himself through the world around him
- *Tarkovsky's Stalker* — a sacred, mysterious space that reveals the person who enters it
- *The Book of Job* — watching a man suffer, and asking what he will do with it

---

## Story

The masked man enters a house familiar in the way dreams are familiar — almost right. He does not know why he came.

The game unfolds across three acts:

**Act I — Arrival:** The house is quiet. The dread is slow. A first memory surfaces, incomplete, like a word on the tip of the tongue.

**Act II — Remembering:** More rooms appear that should not exist. The shadows grow bolder. Two presences make themselves felt: God in the warm rooms, the Devil in the dark corridors. The memories come harder and faster.

**Act III — The Center:** A simple room. Two chairs. A table with something he recognizes. The player makes their final choices. The house answers — not with words, but with everything it has shown him.

---

## The Choice System

Every meaningful decision quietly adjusts a hidden internal scale — not labeled Good or Evil, but leaning toward the answer each presence represents.

Neither path is wrong. The game does not reward or punish. It only observes — the same way the house observes.

---

## Endings

**The God Ending — He Leaves**  
The masked man removes the mask. He sets it down. He walks back through every room, and they are ordinary now. He steps outside and does not look back.

**The Devil Ending — He Stays**  
The masked man reaches the center, looks at the mask, and puts it back on. He sits down. The house settles around him like it has been waiting. He is not trapped. He chose.

*In both endings, God and the Devil appear in the final frame — together, unchanged, watching. The player's choice was never about them.*

## Getting Started

### Prerequisites

- [Godot Engine](https://godotengine.org/download) (version X.X or higher)
- Git

### Installation

```bash
# Clone the repository
git clone https://github.com/Nadirashvil1/andromeda.git
cd andromeda
```

Open the project in Godot:

1. Launch Godot Engine
2. Click **Import** and navigate to the cloned folder
3. Select `project.godot` and open it
4. Press **F5** or click the **Play** button to run the game
